Key Finding: TCF4 regulon targets are completely non-overlapping between mouse and human (0/18, Fisher p=1.0). However, shared functional themes exist:
  • GTPase/RhoGAP signaling: Mouse (ARAP3, ARHGAP27, DOCK4) ↔ Human (ARHGAP18, ARHGAP29, FYN) — same pathway, different effectors
  • Vascular remodeling: Mouse (EGFL7, PLXND1) ↔ Human (APLNR, PDGFB, CD34) — convergent angiogenic function
  • Cell adhesion: Mouse (CCDC85A, PTPRM) ↔ Human (BCAM, DCHS1)

Interpretation: TCF4 is a "conserved TF with divergent targets" — same upstream switch, different downstream effectors shaped by species-specific chromatin landscape and cofactor availability.

Integrated Model: Acute Uniform vs Chronic Selective

Why TCF4 branch-level patterns reverse between species

FactorMouse PAB (Acute)Human RV (Chronic)
Stress timescale Weeks (Sham → 1W → 2W → 4W) Years to decades
Stress distribution Uniform — all ECs hit simultaneously Heterogeneous — variable EC exposure
TCF4 activation Simultaneous, uniform across all ECs (1W peak) Selective — only in decompensated EC subset
Branch difference Non-TCF4 axis (Sema6d/Plxnd1 heterogeneity) TCF4 program IS the bifurcation axis
TCF4 kinetics Hit-and-run: 1W peak → 4W decline Sustained/reactivated in decompensated ECs

Unified Conclusion

TCF4 functions as an RV EC stress response TF in both species:

  • Acute stress (Mouse PAB): All ECs receive the same hit simultaneously → TCF4 activates uniformly → no branch-level enrichment. The hit-and-run kinetics (1W peak) show TCF4 as a transient early switch.
  • Chronic stress (Human RV failure): Years of heterogeneous progression → only some ECs activate TCF4 (decompensated subset) → strong branch-level enrichment. TCF4→APLNR axis drives vascular remodeling in these cells.
  • The branch-level "reversal" is not a contradiction but a natural consequence of acute-uniform vs chronic-selective activation dynamics.
  • TCF4 OE-up signature (bulk validation) is concordantly enriched in disease/RV in both species (strongest evidence of conservation).
